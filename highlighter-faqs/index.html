Highlighter questions answered

Updated June 2026Independently researchedNo paid placement.

Quick answer

The best highlighter for you depends on the paper you use, the look you want, and whether you prize smudge-resistance or vibrancy. Pastel highlighters offer a gentler, modern look without sacrificing readability, while traditional fluorescent shades are still the go-to for maximum contrast. This guide answers the most common questions to help you pick wisely.

What type of highlighter is best for textbooks?

Textbook paper is usually thinner and more absorbent than standard copy paper, so bleed-through and feathering are real concerns. Gel highlighters (like the Pentel Handy-Line Slicci or Zebra Justfit) are a top choice because they lay down a smooth, waxy line that doesn’t soak into the page as quickly as liquid ink. They also tend to dry instantly, which helps avoid smudging when you close the book. If you prefer a liquid highlighter, look for ones labeled “no-bleed” or “dry-to-touch” formulas. The Sharpie Clear View highlighters, for example, let you see a strip of the page through the barrel, so you can accurately place your mark without overshooting. Many owners report that these work well on textbook paper when used with a light hand. For heavy underlining or margin notes, a fine-tip gel pen that doubles as a highlighter might be more practical. Just avoid alcohol-based markers, as they often ghost through to the next page. In short, test a single highlighter on a spare page before committing to a full set.

Are pastel highlighters less visible than neon ones?

Pastel highlighters use softer, desaturated hues like blush pink, lilac, mint, and baby blue. They are less jarring than neon yellow or pink, but they still provide enough contrast against black text to be clearly readable. On white paper, the best pastel formulas achieve that “gentle glow” effect without needing to squint. Visibility depends mostly on the paper color and the specific brand. On off-white or cream paper, some pastel shades can wash out. Owners consistently praise the Mildliner by Zebra for its balanced pastel tones that remain visible on most common paper types. Similarly, the Stabilo Boss Pastel line is well-regarded for holding up even under warm desk lamps. If you’re studying in low light or have vision concerns, fluorescent highlighters will always offer higher contrast. But for everyday note-taking or bullet journaling, pastels are plenty functional, and they avoid that harsh glare that can tire your eyes over long reading sessions.

How do gel highlighters compare to standard liquid highlighters?

Gel highlighters contain a semi-solid, wax-based ink that feels similar to a gel pen. They produce a very smooth, even line with no pooling or streaking. Because the ink sits on the surface of the paper rather than soaking in, gel highlighters bleed through less on thin sheets. They also dry almost instantly, which is a huge plus for left-handed people who often smear liquid ink. On the downside, gel highlighters can smudge if you rub the mark right after applying, and they sometimes skip on glossy or coated paper. They also tend to run out faster than liquid highlighters because the wick is smaller. The Zebra Justfit (often sold in a vivid set) and the Pentel Handy-Line Slicci are two of the most reliable gel options based on user feedback. Liquid highlighters (like the classic Sharpie or Bic Brite Liner) are still the most common for a reason: they’re cheap, last a long time, and come in every color imaginable. But they ghost and bleed more easily. For students who mark up library books or thin paper, gel is the safer bet.

Can highlighters bleed through paper?

Yes, especially if you use a heavy hand or a wet, alcohol-based formula on standard 20-pound printer paper. Highlighters work by delivering dye or pigment dissolved in a solvent. The solvent can soak into the paper fibers and reach the other side, causing ghosting or outright bleed-through. Paper weight, coating, and absorbency all play a role. “No-bleed” formulas are not a marketing gimmick. Brands like Sharpie (with their Clear View line) and Zebra (Mildliner) use less solvent or thicker inks that minimize penetration. Gel highlighters are the most bleed-resistant of all because they sit on top of the paper rather than absorbing. To reduce bleeding regardless of your highlighter: Use a light touch, avoid going over the same spot repeatedly, and choose a brighter shade (which may require less ink to be visible). If you frequently mark both sides of a page, consider a highlighter with a water-based or pigment-based ink, or switch to a gel stick.

What should I look for in a smudge-proof highlighter?

Smudge-proofness is crucial if you plan to write over your highlights with pen or marker. Standard liquid highlighters often smear when you write over them with gel or ballpoint pens, especially if you don’t wait long enough. Smudge-proof highlighters typically use a “dry erase”-like ink that becomes water-resistant once dry, or a gel formula that repels liquid ink. The Stabilo Boss Original (with its known “dry-safe” feature) and the Bic Brite Liner Grip are both popular for staying put under pen strokes. Pastel highlighters like the Mildliner are also widely reported to be smear-resistant when used with fine-liner pens, though they can smear with heavy permanent markers. A good test: after a highlight dries for 30 seconds, draw a line over it with a common ballpoint pen. If the highlighter ink lifts, it’s not suitable for note-taking where you add annotations. Owner forums consistently recommend trying before buying a whole set, or buying a multi-pack sampler first.

Frequently asked questions

Do pastel highlighters work on thin paper?

Yes, generally better than neon or liquid highlighter because their less concentrated dye load and thicker gel formulas reduce bleed. That said, some pastel gels can still ghost on very thin Bible paper or cheap notebook sheets. A light touch helps.

Are there refillable highlighter options?

Yes, several brands offer refillable models. The Stabilo Boss has a refillable “Refillable Highlighter” that takes ink cartridges. The Pilot Frixion Light highlighters are also refillable (and their ink erases with heat). Check if replacement cartridges are available in your region before committing.

Why do some highlighters dry out quickly?

Most highlighters dry out because the cap was left off or because the wick became clogged with dried ink. Alcohol-based markers are especially prone to evaporation. Gel highlighters are less prone because they use wax, but they still dry eventually. To extend life, always recap tightly and store horizontally, point down if possible.

Can I use highlighters on glossy paper?

Standard liquid highlighters often bead up or smear on glossy paper (like magazine pages) because the ink can’t absorb. Gel highlighters work better on glossy surfaces; they adhere like a wax crayon. Some users have good results with the Pentel Handy-Line Slicci for coating glossy pages, but test on a corner first.

What’s the difference between a highlighter and a marker?

Highlighters are designed to be translucent so you can read text through the color. Markers are generally opaque and intended to write or draw over surfaces. Hybrid products like “dual-tip highlighters” (e.g., Tombow Dual Brush) can be used for both, but the ink is usually more pigmented and may hide text if applied heavily.

How do I prevent highlighter smudging in my notebook?

Choose a highlight with a quick-dry formula (gel based is best). Wait at least 10–15 seconds before closing the book. Avoid resting your palm directly on the highlighted area as you write. For left-handed writers, gel sticks or retractable highlighters (like the Pentel Handy-Line) reduce accidental smears.

In shortThe right highlighter makes studying, note-taking, or planning easier and even a little more pleasant. Whether you lean toward the softness of pastels or the punch of neons, understanding how different formulas behave on your paper will save you frustration and wasted money. Pick a few singles to test before committing to a set, and you’ll find the perfect match for your routine.
